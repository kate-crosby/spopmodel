prob_survival {spopmodel}R Documentation

Survival probability by age for San Francisco Estuary-based White Sturgeon.

Description

A dataset for White Sturgeon (Acipenser transmontanus) with 20 ages (0-19) and corresponding survival probability with associated error.

Format

A data frame with 20 rows and 3 variables:

age

numeric age from 0-19

prob

probability of survival at each age

se

error associated with prob

Details

Survival rate estimated using ChapmanRobson(). Value then assigned for ages 3-19. Rate adjusted for exploitation (using Exploitation()) for ages within the slot limit (for this dataset that is ages 10-15, which represents the current slot of 40-60 inches FL). Exploitation for current slot ~13% given 2007-2015 CDFW mark-recapture data.

  • Age 0 survival from Caroffino et al. (2010).

  • Age 1 survival from Pine et al. (2001).

  • Age 2 survival from Ireland et al. (2002).

Source

Model development input data (CDFW; Univ. of Idaho).

References

Caroffino D. C., T. M. Sutton, R. R. Elliott, and M. C. Donofrio. 2010. Early life stage mortality rates of Lake Sturgeon in the Peshtigo River, Wisconsin. North American Journal of Fisheries Management 30:295-304.

Pine, W. E., M. S. Allen, and V. J. Dreitz. 2001. Population viability of the Gulf of Mexico Sturgeon: inferences from capture-recapture and age-structuted models. Transactions of the American Fisheries Society 130:1164-1174.

Ireland, S., C. R. C. Beamesderfer, V. L. Paragamian, V. D. Wakkinen, and J. T. Siple. 2002. Success of hatchery-reared juvenile White Sturgeon (Acipenser transmontanus) following release in the Kootenai River, Idaho USA. Journal of Applied Ichthyology 18:642-650.
